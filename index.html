Program

Credit: ESA/Euclid/Euclid Consortium/NASA, image processing by J.-C. Cuillandre (CEA Paris-Saclay), G. Anselmi

When? July 5–10, 2026

This workshop will bring together members of several Euclid science work packages—including quasars, Lyman-break galaxies, theory, AGN, and galaxy evolution—to discuss and coordinate high-redshift science within the Euclid collaboration. We will also invite members from other scientifically related SWGs and WPs.

The meeting is designed to foster cross-WP interaction, share ongoing work, identify common challenges, and develop collaborative strategies for maximizing Euclid's high-redshift science return. This in-person meeting is essential for addressing data challenges at the precise moment when the landscape of early Universe studies is being defined for the next decade.

This is an invitation-only workshop. If you have received this form, you are invited to participate. We encourage all attendees to come prepared to actively contribute through presentations, discussions, or hands-on sessions.

Registration

Credit: ESA

Registration Deadline: Noon CET, January 19, 2026

Costs: €167.20 per day, covering accommodation, all meals, and use of facilities. Further details are available at the Ringberg Castle website. Payment on-site. Euro-, Master- and Visacard are accepted, but EC-Card and cash are preferred.

Registration is completed by submitting the invitation form.

Venue

Venue and Logistics
Schloss Ringberg, a Max Planck Society conference center overlooking Lake Tegernsee in the Bavarian Alps, offers a quiet and distinctive setting for scientific meetings. If you want to learn more about the venue and its facilities, please visit the Schloss Ringberg website.

Getting There
The castle is reached via Tegernsee, either by regional train from Munich or by car via the A8 motorway. From Tegernsee, a short taxi ride leads up the mountain road to Ringberg. Additional travel guidance is available on the official website.
